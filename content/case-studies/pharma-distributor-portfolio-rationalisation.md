## Project Overview

MedSupply Gujarat Pvt Ltd, a regional pharmaceutical distributor serving 800+ pharmacies across Gujarat, was burdened by a sprawling technology landscape that had grown organically over 12 years. With 14 separate applications and rising IT costs, they needed a strategic approach to rationalise their portfolio and prepare for AI adoption.

## The Challenge

### Fragmented Technology Landscape
- **14 separate applications** accumulated over 12 years without strategic oversight
- **4 inventory tracking tools** across warehousing, procurement, sales, and accounting
- **Legacy DOS-based batch tracking** system critical for drug safety regulation
- **No integration layer** â€” data moved between systems via manual export/import

### Rising Costs with Diminishing Returns
- **â‚¹42 lakhs annual** software licensing and maintenance costs
- **3 full-time employees** dedicated solely to re-keying data between systems
- **Frequent licence audits** revealing significant shelfware across departments
- **Growing compliance risk** from manual processes in a regulated industry

### Missed Modernisation Opportunities
- **No demand forecasting** despite having 5 years of rich sales data
- **Manual compliance reporting** consuming 2 full days each month
- **No mobile access** for field sales representatives
- **Customer service bottlenecks** from inability to check real-time stock

## Our Rationalisation Approach

### Phase 1: Discovery & Inventory (Week 1)
- Catalogued all 14 applications with licence costs, user counts, and business functions
- Conducted stakeholder interviews across 6 departments
- Mapped data flows and identified 23 manual transfer points
- Documented technical debt and compliance risks in legacy systems

### Phase 2: Analysis & Optimisation (Weeks 2-3)
- Scored each application on business value, technical health, and cost efficiency
- Identified 4 redundant systems with overlapping capabilities
- Modelled 3 consolidation scenarios with projected costs and risks
- Benchmarked against industry-standard pharma distribution platforms

### Phase 3: AI Opportunity Mapping (Week 4)
- Analysed daily business processes for automation potential
- Identified 5 high-impact AI use cases:
  1. **Demand forecasting** using historical sales and seasonal patterns
  2. **Automated compliance reporting** with data aggregation
  3. **Intelligent stock reordering** based on consumption trends
  4. **Expiry date management** with automated FEFO optimisation
  5. **Customer order prediction** for proactive outreach

### Phase 4: Strategy & Roadmap (Week 5)
- Delivered comprehensive rationalisation strategy
- Created phased migration plan prioritising quick wins
- Defined KPIs and success metrics for each phase
- Provided vendor evaluation framework for replacement systems
